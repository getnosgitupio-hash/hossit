◆ Quick Answers

The Questions every operator asks first.

What does a Scope Call actually do?
30 minutes on Zoom. You bring a one paragraph description of your core workflow and a screenshot of your current SaaS stack. We calculate your Workaround Tax in writing, identify the 3 workflows custom software would replace first, and give you a go or no-go verdict with reasons. If we say no-go, we say why. Zero pitch, zero pressure.
How much does a full custom software build cost?
For a v1 with 8 to 13 features, fixed scope, 60-day delivery: between ₹10,86,787 and ₹29,65,800. The exact number is locked at the end of week 2 of the build, in writing, before you commit beyond the scoping sprint.
What if I do not know exactly what I need built?
That is what week 1 is for. The scoping sprint produces a PRD, wireframes, and a tech architecture document. You walk away with all three even if you decide not to continue. The scoping sprint is paid (₹73,400 to ₹1,87,000 depending on complexity) and is the only commitment you make before the fixed-price quote arrives.
Will it actually integrate with my existing tools?
Yes, natively, two way. Tally, Zoho, GST APIs, Razorpay, Stripe, WhatsApp Business API, Slack, Gmail, and most SaaS tools with public APIs. If a tool has no API, we engineer around it (CSV import/export pipelines, scheduled sync, etc). The integrations are scoped explicitly in week 2.
Who owns the code, the data, and the credentials on day 60?
You. All of it. Source code in your GitHub organisation. Hosting in your AWS or Vercel account, not ours. Database credentials, API keys, every login, every domain. The handover is signed off in writing on day 60. There is no scenario in which you cannot fire us and keep running.
Why does Hoisst turn down 40% of Scope Calls?
Because the 60-day promise only holds when the fit is right. If you need 30 features in v1, our build is not for you. If your scope is genuinely a one-week freelancer job, our overhead is too high. We say no on the call so that when we say yes, we mean it.
